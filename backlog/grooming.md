# Backlog Grooming Guide

A structured process for keeping your backlog healthy, prioritized,
and ready for sprint planning.

## Overview

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Once per week |
| Duration | 1 hour max |
| Attendees | PM, Engineering lead, Designer |
| Output | Top 10 items sprint-ready |

---

## What is backlog grooming?

Grooming is the process of reviewing, refining, and prioritizing
backlog items so the team is never blocked waiting for clarity.

> A well-groomed backlog means sprint planning takes 1 hour, not 3.

---

## The grooming checklist

For every item in the top 10, verify:

- [ ] Title is clear and specific
- [ ] Description explains the WHY not just the WHAT
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are written
- [ ] Dependencies are identified
- [ ] Item is estimated or ready to estimate
- [ ] Priority is assigned (High / Med / Low)

---

## Prioritization framework — RICE scoring

| Factor | Question | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Reach | How many users affected? | 1-10 |
| Impact | How much does it move the needle? | 1-10 |
| Confidence | How sure are we? | 1-10 |
| Effort | How much work? (lower = better) | 1-10 |

**RICE Score = (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort**

Higher score = higher priority.

---

## Item lifecycle
Idea → Backlog → Groomed → Sprint Ready → In Progress → Done

An item is "Sprint Ready" only when it passes the grooming checklist.

---

## User story format
As a [type of user]
I want to [do something]
So that [I get some benefit]

Example:
As a first-time user
I want to complete onboarding in under 5 minutes
So that I can start using the product without frustration

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Grooming in sprint planning — too late
- Items without acceptance criteria — causes rework
- Backlog over 50 items — becomes unmanageable
- No RICE scoring — leads to HiPPO decisions
  (Highest Paid Person's Opinion)
