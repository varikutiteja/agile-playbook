# Sprint Planning Guide

A step-by-step guide to running effective sprint planning sessions.

## Overview

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Every 2 weeks |
| Duration | 2 hours max |
| Attendees | PM, Engineering lead, Designer, QA |
| Output | Committed sprint backlog |

---

## Before the meeting (PM's job)

- [ ] Backlog is groomed and prioritized
- [ ] Top 10 items have clear acceptance criteria
- [ ] Dependencies are identified and flagged
- [ ] Capacity is calculated (who is available this sprint)
- [ ] Previous sprint velocity is reviewed

---

## During the meeting (60 min)

### Part 1 — Sprint goal (10 min)
Define the ONE thing this sprint must achieve.

Example:
> "By end of sprint, users can complete onboarding without 
> contacting support."

### Part 2 — Backlog review (30 min)
- Walk through top priority items
- Engineering estimates each item in story points
- PM clarifies requirements if questions arise
- Remove or defer anything that can't be completed

### Part 3 — Commitment (20 min)
- Team commits to items that fit within capacity
- Each item gets an owner
- Risks and blockers are flagged upfront

---

## Story point scale

| Points | Effort |
|---|---|
| 1 | Trivial — under 2 hours |
| 2 | Small — half a day |
| 3 | Medium — 1 day |
| 5 | Large — 2-3 days |
| 8 | Very large — needs breaking down |
| 13 | Too big — must split before sprint |

---

## After the meeting (PM's job)

- [ ] Update JIRA/Trello with committed items
- [ ] Send sprint goal to all stakeholders
- [ ] Set up daily standup invite
- [ ] Document any risks identified

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Committing to more than velocity allows
- Starting planning without groomed backlog
- Skipping the sprint goal — it keeps the team aligned
- Letting planning run over 2 hours
